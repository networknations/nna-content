Explores cutting-edge issues associated with digital sovereignty, drawing on theories of political economy, international law, human rights, and data protection. Examines how new technological developments in AI, e-commerce, and the sharing economy have posed challenges to assertion of digital sovereignty. Presents the contrast between territorial and [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]] approaches to data governance — a key theoretical distinction for [[concepts/network-nations|Network Nations]].
