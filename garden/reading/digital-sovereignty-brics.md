**Published:** Cambridge University Press

Examines how BRICS nations assert digital sovereignty — the exercise of control over the Internet. As governments worldwide answer the question of *who* should regulate the Internet (they all will), the second-generation question becomes *how*. This volume provides context for understanding state-level digital sovereignty claims, against which the [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]] of [[concepts/network-nations|Network Nations]] operates.
