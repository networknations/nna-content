A Web3 organization and platform founded by [[people/kevin-owocki|Kevin Owocki]], focused on funding open-source software and digital public goods through quadratic funding mechanisms. One of the most significant experiments in [[concepts/commons|commons]]-based resource allocation in the blockchain space, Gitcoin has distributed hundreds of millions of dollars to public goods projects through its grants rounds.

Gitcoin's quadratic funding mechanism (where the number of contributors matters as much as the amount contributed) operationalizes the [[concepts/stake-based-governance|stake-based governance]] principle of Network Nations: influence and resource allocation tied to community participation rather than capital concentration. The "Gitcoin Passport" — a verifiable identity and reputation system — is referenced in the essay as an example of multi-positional digital citizenship.
