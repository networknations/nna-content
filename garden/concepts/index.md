This page serves as an entry point into the foundational ideas that define [[concepts/network-nations|Network Nations]]. The concepts below are drawn from the ten guiding principles and the core definitional cards of the Network Nations Alliance.

## Foundational Principles

Each principle follows a "Neither X nor Y but Z" pattern, rejecting false dichotomies in favour of a third way:

1. [[concepts/translocalism|Translocalism]] — Neither Local Nor Global But Translocal
2. [[concepts/polycentrism|Polycentrism]] — Neither Centralized Nor Decentralised But Polycentric
3. [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|Functional Sovereignty]] — Neither Supremacy Nor Subordination But Functional Sovereignty
4. [[concepts/commons|Commons]] — Neither State Nor Market But Commons
5. [[concepts/interdependence|Interdependence]] — Neither Dependent Nor Independent But Interdependent
6. [[concepts/stake-based-governance|Stake-Based Governance]] — Neither Exit-Based Nor Voice-Based But Stake-Based
7. [[concepts/metapolitics|Metapolitics]] — Neither Political Nor Apolitical But Metapolitical
8. [[concepts/beyonders|Beyonders]] — Neither Insiders Nor Outsiders But Beyonders
9. [[concepts/techno-realism|Techno-Realism]] — Neither Techno-Utopian Nor Techno-Dystopian But Techno-Realists
10. [[concepts/emergent-solutions|Emergent Solutions]] — Neither Descriptive Nor Prescriptive But Emergent
