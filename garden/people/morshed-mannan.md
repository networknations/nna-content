Legal researcher and co-author of *Blockchain Governance* (MIT Press, 2024) alongside [[people/primavera-de-filippi|Primavera De Filippi]] and Wouter Reijers. Also co-author of "The Alegality of Blockchain Technology" (*Policy and Society*, 2022). His research addresses the legal and regulatory dimensions of decentralized organizations, particularly the gap between blockchain-based governance and existing state law.

In the episode on [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]], Mannan brings legal precision to the question of how Network Nations can exercise meaningful autonomy within — and alongside — existing legal frameworks. His work on "alegality" — the idea that blockchain-based systems operate neither legally nor illegally, but in a space the law has not yet reached — is particularly relevant to the concept of functional sovereignty as operational freedom.
