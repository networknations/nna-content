Research Director at CNRS (French National Centre for Scientific Research) and Faculty Associate at Harvard Berkman Klein Center. Co-host of the Network Nations podcast series alongside [[people/felix-beer|Felix Beer]], and co-author of the [[essay|Network Nations essay]]. A leading scholar on blockchain governance, digital commons, and [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]].

Her academic work includes the books *Blockchain and the Law* (with Aaron Wright, 2018) and *Blockchain Governance* (MIT Press, 2024, with Reijers and Mannan). She is part of the [[organizations/blockchain-gov|Blockchain.gov]] European research project, which frames the Network Nations series. Her thinking draws on institutional theory, commons scholarship, and the practical governance of decentralized communities.

The concept of [[concepts/network-nations|Network Nations]] emerged organically from her years of research on intentional communities, cloud community theory, blockchain-based virtual nations, and translocal governance — catalyzed by a reaction to Balaji Srinivasan's *The Network State*.
