Legal scholar who has written extensively on [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]] and the ethics of technology platforms. His work examines how large technology companies exercise sovereign-like power over their users, and the implications for democratic governance and human rights.

His analysis of functional sovereignty — how non-state actors acquire and exercise governing power — provides important theoretical grounding for understanding both the risks and possibilities of [[concepts/network-nations|Network Nations]].
