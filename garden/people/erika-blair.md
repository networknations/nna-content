Practitioner deeply embedded in the Burning Man community and ecosystem, contributing to the episode that examines Burning Man as a case study — or "seed" — for Network Nations thinking. The Burning Man community is one of the primary empirical examples [[people/primavera-de-filippi|Primavera De Filippi]] and [[people/felix-beer|Felix Beer]] use to illustrate what a mature, [[concepts/translocalism|translocal]] community with strong [[concepts/collective-identity|collective identity]] looks like.

Blair brings insider perspective on how Burning Man's network of regional burns operates — each node maintaining local specificity while sharing the same cultural principles — and on the community's evolving desire for greater collective governance and mutualization beyond individual events. The "Burner" identity as a self-assigned collective label, and the community's exploration of deeper [[concepts/self-governance|self-governance]], make it a key reference point for the Network Nations project.

**Appears in:** [[podcast/ep10-burning-man|Ep 10: Burning Man — Seeding a Network Nation]]
