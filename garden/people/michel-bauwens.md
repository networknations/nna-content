Founder of the [[organizations/p2p-foundation|P2P Foundation]] (founded 2005) and one of the most influential theorists of the commons economy. Over three decades, he has mapped and supported communities around the globe that use peer-to-peer networks to create and share value collectively. Author of several landmark works on commons and peer production, and writes a newsletter called *The Fourth Generation Civilization*.

Bauwens introduces the concept of the "procession of the commons" — the historical cycle in which strong states and markets weaken commons-based solidarity, while periods of state decline revive the need for direct mutual aid. He proposes three geopolitical models shaping the future: the Western WEF model (multi-stakeholder without popular sovereignty), the BRICS civilizational-state model (popular sovereignty but authoritarian), and a third way — the "archipelago of regenerative villages" — grounded in cosmo-localism and [[concepts/translocalism|translocal]] solidarity networks.

He introduces the concept of the "cosmo-local financing facility" — attracting floating capital toward regenerative community investment — and argues that entanglement (shared capital, shared identity, and mutual mobility) is the mechanism by which [[concepts/commons|commons]] communities can build durable transnational networks. He believes blockchain has functioned, paradoxically, as a binding ideology for networked communities even when its technical implementations are imperfect.
