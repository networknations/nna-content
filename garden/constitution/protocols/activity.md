This protocol governs how self-organized activities emerge, operate, and conclude within the Network Nations Alliance.

## Instantiation

**Trigger Type:** Action-based

**Trigger Condition:**
- A Member proposes a new activity
- An existing activity requires transition or conclusion

## Authority & Oversight

| Role | Authority | Accountability |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| [[constitution/structure/roles/activity-coordinator|Activity Coordinators]] | Coordinates their activity | Steward Council, participants |
| [[constitution/structure/groups/steward-council|Steward Council]] | Supports activities, provides resources | Alliance Assembly |
| Activity Participants | Contribute to activity | Activity Coordinator |

## What Are Activities?

Activities are self-organized efforts that advance Alliance purpose. They include:

- **Mapping projects:** Researching and documenting Network Nations landscape
- **Alliances:** Building relationships with aligned organizations
- **Initiatives:** Focused efforts toward specific outcomes
- **Grants:** Funding programs supporting Network Nations development
- **Gatherings:** Events bringing community together
- **Courses:** Educational programs on Network Nations topics
- **Other:** Any effort aligned with Alliance purpose

Activities are:
- **Self-organized:** Emerge from Member initiative, not top-down direction
- **Aligned:** Serve Alliance purpose and uphold Alliance values
- **Supported:** May receive Alliance resources and visibility
- **Accountable:** Report to Alliance on progress and outcomes

## Starting an Activity

### Proposal Requirements

A Member proposing an activity provides:
- **Name:** Clear, descriptive title
- **Purpose:** How it serves Alliance purpose
- **Scope:** What it will do and not do
- **Duration:** Expected timeline (ongoing or time-bound)
- **Support needed:** What resources or support requested
- **Coordinator:** Who will lead (usually the proposer)

### Formation Process

| Step | Action | Actor | Duration | Output |
|------|--------|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Draft proposal | Proposer | -- | Proposal shared |
| 2 | Gather support | Proposer | -- | 2+ Members express support |
| 3 | Announce | Proposer | -- | Activity announced to Alliance |
| 4 | Launch | Activity Coordinator | -- | Activity begins |

**Support requirement:** Two Members (besides proposer) must express support for the activity. This ensures activities have initial momentum and aren't purely solo efforts.

**Announcement:** Activities are announced via Alliance communication channels. No approval required from Steward Council, but Council is notified.

## Operating an Activity

### Activity Coordinator Responsibilities

- Coordinate participants and working sessions
- Maintain communication with Alliance
- Ensure alignment with Alliance purpose and values
- Track progress and share updates

### Participant Responsibilities

- Contribute as committed
- Uphold Alliance values in activity spaces
- Communicate availability and constraints

### Alliance Support

Activities may request from Steward Council:
- Platform access or dedicated channels
- Announcement and visibility support
- Financial resources (per [[constitution/protocols/treasury-management|Treasury Management Protocol]])
- Connections to relevant parties

Steward Council decides on resource requests per [[constitution/protocols/steward-council|Steward Council Protocol]].

## Activity Updates

**Quarterly:** Activity Coordinator provides brief status update (at Alliance Assembly or async)
**At milestones:** Significant outcomes or changes shared with Alliance
**As needed:** Questions or support requests to Steward Council

## Concluding an Activity

Activities may conclude in several ways:

### Successful Completion

- Activity achieves its purpose
- Lead documents outcomes and lessons learned
- Final report shared with Alliance
- Materials archived

### Transition

- Activity Coordinator hands off to successor
- New Lead continues per [[constitution/protocols/activity-coordinator|Activity Coordinator Protocol]]
- Alliance notified of transition

### Pause

- Activity becomes dormant but not concluded
- Lead notifies Alliance of pause
- May reactivate later

### Wind-Down

- Activity determines it's no longer viable or valuable
- Lead communicates wind-down decision
- Materials archived, participants thanked

### Steward Council Intervention

If an activity:
- Becomes misaligned with Alliance purpose
- Causes harm or conflict
- Becomes inactive without communication

Steward Council may:
- Reach out to Activity Coordinator for clarification
- Request course correction
- Archive activity after 6 months of inactivity

## Completion & Outputs

**Protocol completes when:**
- Activity concludes (any pathway)
- Activity transitions to new Lead

**Outputs:**
- Activity record and documentation
- Final report (for concluded activities)
- Archived materials

## Exceptions & Edge Cases

- **Multiple Leads:** Activities may have co-leads; they coordinate among themselves
- **Overlapping activities:** Similar activities may coexist; collaboration encouraged but not required
- **Large activities:** Activities with significant scope may develop their own governance with Steward Council input
