A structural analysis of the political economy of digital platforms and the emerging landscape of governance experimentation, examining where Network Nations fit within the broader terrain of political alternatives.

---

## Key Themes

- Platform capitalism and the structural dynamics of digital monopoly (drawing on [[people/nick-srnicek|Srnicek]]'s *Platform Capitalism*)
- The political landscape of progressive vs. techno-libertarian responses to the polycrisis
- How the collapse of post-war institutional narratives is opening space for governance innovation
- What a genuinely progressive "tech politics" would look like — beyond regulation and beyond libertarian exit
- [[concepts/metapolitics|Metapolitical]] dimensions of Network Nations: designing a new political environment rather than advocating a political program
- The role of [[concepts/commons|digital commons]] and cooperative alternatives in reshaping the political economy of networks
