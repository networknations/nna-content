Exploring "entanglement" as a design principle for [[concepts/network-nations|Network Nations]] — how communities build voluntary [[concepts/interdependence|interdependencies]] through shared capital, shared identity systems, and mutual mobility across nodes. Asks: what makes a network of communities more than the sum of its parts?

---

## Key Themes

- **Entanglement** as a designed quality of network communities — not just connection but mutual commitment and shared stakes
- The distinction between [[concepts/translocalism|translocal]] coordination (many places doing similar things) and genuine entanglement (shared resources, identity, and vulnerability)
- [[people/jon-hillis|Jon Hillis]]'s work at [[organizations/cabin|Cabin]] as a case study in building physical-digital hybrid network infrastructure
- How "functional local and network sovereignties" complement each other: locally-rooted nodes linked by networked governance
- Historical analogies: Medieval guild networks, the Republic of Letters, ecovillage passport systems
- The question of appropriate capital — how entangled communities attract and govern shared investment

## Concepts Introduced

- **Entanglement** — voluntary [[concepts/interdependence|interdependencies]] created through shared capital, shared identity infrastructure, and mutual mobility across community nodes
- **Functional local sovereignty** — governance authority rooted in specific places
- **Network sovereignty** — governance authority distributed across connected nodes
