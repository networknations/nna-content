A deep dive into [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]] — one of the core concepts of Network Nations — with two scholars who bring legal and constitutional theory perspectives to the question of how communities can exercise meaningful autonomy within and alongside existing state structures.

---

## Key Themes

- The legal and theoretical grounding of [[concepts/functional-sovereignty|functional sovereignty]] as distinct from territorial sovereignty
- [[people/morshed-mannan|Morshed Mannan]]'s work on "alegality" — the space blockchain-based governance occupies that is neither legal nor illegal under existing frameworks
- [[people/neil-walker|Neil Walker]]'s constitutional pluralism — how multiple overlapping sovereign claims coexist without hierarchy
- How Network Nations can claim operational autonomy in specific domains without challenging state authority
- The relationship between functional sovereignty and democratic legitimacy
- Practical domains where Network Nations are already exercising functional sovereignty: identity systems, community currencies, mutual aid, dispute resolution
